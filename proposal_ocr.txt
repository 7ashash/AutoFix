Project Title 1. Intelligent System for Auto Parts Management & Ordering 2. Introduction / Background In Egypt, the automotive spare parts market faces several challenges that affect both car owners and dealers. Many users struggle to find reliable sources for original spare parts, often ending up with counterfeit or Iow-quality alternatives. This problem is intensified by the lack of centralized platforms that connect users directly with trusted dealers. Moreover, there is limited price transparency and difficulty in comparing between original and aftermarket parts. Users often rely on social media groups or informal markets, which increases the risk of fraud and misinformation. Dealers, on the other hand, face difficulties in reaching potential customers and managing their inventory efficiently. These challenges highlight the need for a smart, centralized digital platform that can connect users and dealers, ensure authenticity verification, and simplify the spare parts purchasing process. This project demonstrates how modern Information Technology can solve real-world market problems through digital transformation. The Spare Parts Hub leverages several key IT domains — including web development, artificial intelligence, natural language processing (NLP), database management, and cybersecurity — to build a comprehensive and intelligent platform. By integrating Al-powered chatbots, authenticity verification systems, and data-driven analytics, the project showcases how IT can enhance user experience, automate processes, and improve trust and transparency in e-commerce. Additionally, the system's design promotes scalability, security, and interoperability, reflecting current trends in cloud computing, API integration, and Al-enhanced customer service. Therefore, this topic is highly relevant to the IT field as it combines technical innovation with practical application in the automotive industry.

The absence of a smart, centralized, and interactive platform that connects users, dealers, and service providers results in communication gaps, lack of trust, and inefficient maintenance processes. 6 . Proposed Solution / System Overview General Description 1. 2. The proposed solution is a web-based intelligent platform that connects car owners with spare-parts dealers in Egypt. The platform aims to provide a trusted and efficient digital marketplace for both original and aftermarket car parts. It integrates Al-powered search, authenticity verification, and online ordering to simplify the spare-parts purchasing process while ensuring transparency and user satisfaction. The system bridges the communication gap between users and dealers by allowing both sides to interact through an intuitive interface, ensuring reliable transactions and reducing the risk of counterfeit products. Additionally, the platform extends its functionality beyond e-commerce by integrating intelligent diagnostic support, making it a complete digital assistant for car owners. The Al Chatbot in the Spare Parts Hub system now includes two intelligent modules designed to improve both the shopping and maintenance experience for users: Spare Parts Search Assistant: Helps users find spare parts based on the car model, type, or part name. The chatbot interacts with the user in Arabic or English (including slang) and retrieves relevant products directly from the platform's database. This feature simplifies the search process and ensures users quickly find suitable parts. Car Fault Diagnosis Assistant: This module assists users who are facing car issues. The chatbot asks a series of interactive questions to identify the possible cause of the problem. Once the fault is diagnosed, the system automatically: o Displays a list of nearby maintenance centers with their contact details. o Allows the user to call the center directly or request a mechanic for on-site repair if the issue is minor. o If the issue is serious, the chatbot suggests sending a tow truck (winch) to transport the car to the nearest service center. This dual-function chatbot enhances the system's intelligence and practicality, transforming it from a simple search tool into a virtual automotive assistant capable of both e-commerce and diagnostic support.

How the System Works : 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. 6. User Access: The user visits the platform and interacts either through the main search bar or the intelligent chatbot. AI Chatbot Interaction: Spare Parts Search Mode: When users request parts by model, brand, or category, the chatbot retrieves relevant items from the database or Google API. Car Fault Diagnosis Mode: When users report a car issue, the chatbot asks guided questions to identify the potential fault and then recommends nearby service centers or support options. Parts Display: The system shows both original and aftermarket parts with prices, ratings, and availability. Fake Parts Detection: Users can enter a serial number to verify the authenticity of spare parts through the system's validation feature. Ordering & Payment: Users can add selected parts to their shopping cart, place orders, and choose between delivery or pickup. Dealer Management: Dealers can log in, manage their inventory, update prices, and view analytics on sales and search trends through the dashboard. This integrated workflow creates a complete digital ecosystem for the Egyptian automotive industry — combining intelligent part search, fault diagnosis, and maintenance support into one unified platform.

7. Methodology Development Approach The project follows the Agile development methodology, which allows for flexibility and continuous improvement throughout the development process. The system will be developed in iterative sprints, where each sprint focuses on delivering specific features — such as the chatbot, dealer management, and ordering system — followed by testing and feedback. This approach ensures faster development, better collaboration among team members, and easier adaptation to changing requirements. Data Collection, Research, and Design Method Data and insights for the project were gathered through market research, online surveys, and interviews with local car owners and spare parts dealers. The team analyzed common problems such as counterfeit parts, price inconsistency, and poor online availability. Based on this research, user requirements were defined, and system design was created using UML diagrams, ERD models, and Figma prototypes to visualize the interface and functionality before development.
